Στοιχεία Πιθανοτήτων

The textbook is intended for teaching the basic theory of probability and developing some of its fundamental applications in statistics and computer science. It is primarily aimed at first-year students in departments of computer science, mathematics, and statistics, but it is also suitable for engineering students due to the wide range of topics covered. The content includes key concepts such as probability events, axiomatic foundations, major distributions, discrete and continuous random variables, the law of large numbers, and the central limit theorem. The book is ideal for programs with limited mathematical background, helping students understand the distinct mindset of probability theory compared to other mathematical subjects.

Authors: Κοντογιάννης Ιωάννης, Τουμπής Σταύρος

Pages: 475

Rating: 4.7/5 (124)


Probability and Statistics

This textbook combines probability theory with statistical inference, offering a thorough introduction to both subjects. It covers topics such as probability distributions, expectation, estimation, hypothesis testing, and regression analysis. The book is suitable for advanced undergraduate and graduate students and is often used in courses that blend probability with statistical applications.

Authors: Morris H. DeGroot and Mark J. Schervish

Pages: 798

Rating: 4.2/5 (531)


Stochastic Processes

This book provides an introduction to stochastic processes, with a focus on Markov chains, Poisson processes, and continuous-time processes. It includes applications in fields like operations research, computer science, and finance. The book strikes a balance between theory and application, making it accessible to students who are new to the subject but want a deeper understanding of stochastic models.

Author: Sheldon Ross

Pages: 315

Rating: 4.4/5 (2498)
